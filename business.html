how it works

Your data improves every day the platform grows.

Qoyl's consumer platform collects structured outcome data from day one. The more consumers use it, the richer the brand intelligence becomes.

01
Consumers build profiles
Hair type, porosity, scalp condition, thickness, climate, and water hardness — captured at onboarding and updated over time.
→
02
Products get scanned
Consumers paste ingredient lists. Every scan resolves to structured ingredient data and logs a compatibility result against their profile.
→
03
Outcomes are logged
After each wash day, consumers log moisture, protein, and scalp results. Every log is a labeled data point — real signal, not sentiment.
→
04
Your dashboard updates
Brand dashboards reflect aggregate consumer behaviour across your product range — refreshed continuously as new data comes in.
why brands trust qoyl

Algorithm-first integrity. Always.

Your products are never filtered in or out of recommendations based on your subscription tier. The algorithm scores every ingredient on science — partnership only accelerates our review of your formulations.

Built by a biochemist. Qoyl's ingredient database was designed by a systems engineer with professional experience in pharmaceutical and food industry formulation analysis.
No editorial scoring. Every compatibility score is computed algorithmically from structured ingredient properties — porosity, molecular weight, solubility, pH range, and documented conflict pairs.
Brand partners don't get special ratings. Paying for a dashboard gives you access to data about your product's performance — it does not change what that performance is.
Real outcomes, not panels. Qoyl's data comes from consumers who actively use the platform and log their own results — not paid research participants answering surveys.
ML roadmap at 1,000 users. At scale, Qoyl's collaborative filtering model will surface which specific product formulations generate the best real-world outcomes — across every hair type in the database.
Consumer data is anonymised. Individual consumer profiles are never shared with brand partners. Dashboards surface aggregate patterns — never personally identifiable data.
