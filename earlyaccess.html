Type 4 curl pattern guide
TYPE 4A
4a — Defined Coils
Tight S-shaped coils, about the width of a crochet needle. Defined pattern when wet, some shrinkage.
TYPE 4B
4b — Z-Pattern
Sharp Z-shaped bends, less defined curl. Fluffy and full. High shrinkage — up to 70% of actual length.
TYPE 4C
4c — Tightest Coil
Tightest Z-pattern, little to no visible curl definition without styling. Shrinkage up to 75%+ of actual length.
Not sure which type you are?
Look at a single curl when your hair is freshly washed with no product. 4a will form a loose spiral on its own. 4b will bend in Z-shapes but not spiral. 4c will clump with other strands but won't form a defined pattern on its own. Many people have more than one type — choose the most dominant one, or select the mix option above.
The float test — how to find your porosity
Take a clean shed hair — no product on it — and drop it in a glass of room-temperature water. Wait 4 minutes and look where it sits.
LOW POROSITY
Cuticles tightly closed
Hair repels water. Takes long to get wet, slow to dry. Product tends to sit on top rather than absorb.
common signs: water beads on hair · product buildup · takes forever to dry
MEDIUM POROSITY
Cuticles balanced
Hair absorbs moisture well and holds it. The most forgiving porosity — most routines work well.
common signs: dries in reasonable time · holds styles well · responds to most products
HIGH POROSITY
Cuticles have gaps
Hair absorbs moisture fast but loses it just as fast. Often caused by damage, color, or heat.
common signs: dries fast · frizz · tangles easily · color fades quickly
What your cuticle actually looks like
Low — tight, sealed
Medium — slightly open
High — lifted, gaps
Color treated, bleached, or heat-damaged hair is almost always high porosity. If your hair soaks up product instantly but always feels dry — that's high porosity too.
The thread test — how to find your strand thickness
Take a single shed hair and hold it next to a piece of regular sewing thread. Compare the width of the two.
Fine
Delicate, breaks easily. Needs lightweight products — avoid heavy butters and thick oils.
Medium
Average thickness. Most products work. Balance between lightweight and rich formulas.
Coarse
Strong and resilient. Can handle heavier butters, oils, and rich conditioning treatments.
Note — strand thickness is different from hair density (how much hair you have). You can have fine strands and a lot of them, or coarse strands and very few. Thickness refers to the individual strand only.
